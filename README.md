## What Makes a VPS Black Friday Deal Actually Worth It?

Here's the honest truth: most VPS deals during Black Friday fall into one of three categories.

**The good ones** — genuine discounts off regular pricing, often with recurring (not just first-term) savings locked in. These are rare but they exist.

**The meh ones** — 50% off for the first three months, then full price forever. Great for a trial, terrible if you're building something real.

**The trap ones** — "Up to 80% off" in the headline, but the discounted plan has 512MB RAM and 10GB of storage from 2015-era hardware. The specs wouldn't run a modern Node.js app, let alone anything production-grade.

So what should you actually look for when hunting vps black friday deals?

1. **Recurring discounts** — One-time savings feel nice for thirty seconds. Lifetime discount codes that lock in your price permanently? That's money in your pocket every renewal cycle.
2. **Hardware quality** — AMD EPYC processors and NVMe SSD storage are table stakes now. If a deal doesn't mention the hardware, that's usually a reason to ask why.
3. **Network quality** — For anyone serving users in Asia or running latency-sensitive workloads, the network matters more than the raw specs. A $3/month VPS with 300ms ping to your users is worth less than a $15/month VPS with 15ms ping.
4. **Bandwidth policy** — "Unlimited bandwidth" claims are marketing. What actually matters is: what happens when you hit the limit? Throttled-but-still-running beats hard cutoff every time.
